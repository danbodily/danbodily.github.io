About Dan

Solve-A-Crossword

While I've been solving puzzles for years, it was only recently that I tried my hand at constructing. As a constructor, I employ a unique construction method, relying on both commercially available software and homegrown code to help me build out and color my grids (see more here). In 2020, I constructed a puzzle while sitting at an overlook of the Grand Canyon, waiting for my wife to complete her personal goal of going rim-to-rim-to-rim (40+ miles!). Oddly enough, that particular puzzle would later become my debut puzzle in the New York Times. Nothing like the great outdoors to really provide some inspiration!

I hope you'll take a minute and try out one of my puzzles below. Feel free to subscribe if you'd like to receive more puzzles directly in your inbox.

On a final note, I always welcome collaboration with newer constructors. If you'd like to get into constructing (or if you're already constructing and would like to coop an idea) shoot me an email at danbodily@gmail.com.

The Sunnyside

Aug, 2020

About This Puzzle: When I first started constructing, I submitted a lot of puzzles quickly, suspecting none of them would make the cut. My focus was to get feedback, learn quickly, and not prematurely optimize poorly framed puzzles that never had a shot at getting published to begin with. I learned a lot over many months. This puzzle was my 10th or so that I constructed and it was about at this point that I started to feel I was learning the basic ropes of constructing. For this puzzle, I took a lot of inspiration from the first puzzle I ever wrote (see below). While not my best work, I felt this puzzle offered a powerful "AHA!" moment to the solver. I hope you get a kick out of it.

Months after constructing this puzzle, I found this New York Times puzzle and realized I hadn't been the first to run after this idea. It was disappointing in the moment, but I've since come to realize that this experience is actually pretty common among constructors of all levels. Finding a unique, catchy, original theme is really the biggest challenge in building these puzzles. Even when you do find something that seems original, it seems you can never "be sure" someone somewhere hasn't done it before.

A Hidden Drawer

April, 2020

About This Puzzle: This was my first puzzle ever constructed! Even though I see some major faults with it now, I was and still am proud of it as my first attempt. "DXD" was a big offender (26-Across), being a rather contrived abbreviation. Keep in mind I was using Phil at this time to construct puzzles and with the constraints forced in the top left corner by the theme, I was pretty much toast. Later I looked at this corner with more advanced software and didn't find a clean solution given my constraints.

Paul, my brother, helped me find the clever title and polish some of the clues.
